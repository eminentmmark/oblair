About Oyugi Blair

Blair is an Information Technology and Data Systems professional committed to strengthening humanitarian and development work through structured data, ethical digital systems, and evidence-based decision-making.

His work focuses on building reliable digital infrastructure that supports programme delivery, improves accountability, and enhances the visibility of impact. With a background in Information Technology and advanced certifications in Data Analysis, Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, Prompt Engineering, and Cybersecurity, he combines technical expertise with a strong appreciation for governance, safeguarding, and operational discipline.

Currently serving as a Data Associate within a women-focused NGO, Blair oversees data governance, beneficiary database management, website development, digital compliance, and impact reporting systems. He supports the ethical collection and analysis of qualitative and quantitative data, ensuring informed consent, privacy protection, and alignment with safeguarding standards. His role bridges field realities with structured reporting—transforming lived experiences into data that informs strategy and funding decisions.

Beyond data systems, he contributes to digital storytelling and communications by strengthening online presence, supporting proposal development, and producing responsible visual documentation that respects dignity and cultural context. He recognizes that in humanitarian and development work, data is not merely numerical—it represents people, vulnerability, resilience, and opportunity.

Core Strengths

  • Designing and strengthening data collection and monitoring systems
  • Implementing data cleaning, validation, and quality control mechanisms
  • Supporting programme reporting and donor-aligned documentation
  • Enhancing operational efficiency through structured digital workflows
  • Applying AI tools responsibly to improve productivity and analysis
  • Upholding data protection, safeguarding, and governance standards

    • Blair's Approach

    Blair believes effective humanitarian response and development programming depend on credible data, coordinated operations, and ethical technology use. His work is guided by integrity, inclusion, accountability, and empathy—ensuring that digital systems serve communities rather than overshadow them.

    He is particularly motivated by roles that support human flourishing, literacy, recovery and resilience, and, community empowering. Blair brings both technical discipline and a service-oriented mindset to every partnership.
